A brief history

1987
First planting — The Casterra family establishes the retreat with a small orchard and vegetable plot.
1995
Heritage seed collection — Begins with regional heirlooms; now over 200 varieties.
2005
No-till adoption — A turning point: we commit to soil health, eliminating tillage entirely.
2016
Briarpath journal — Online publication launched, sharing gardening essays and research.
2026
40th season — Continuing the legacy with renewed focus on climate-adapted gardening.

Philosophy & practice

At Briarpath, we see gardening as a conversation with the land. Our methods are rooted in observation: we listen to the soil, the weather, and the plants themselves. We favor perennials, minimal disturbance, and diversity. Every bed is a living experiment in resilience. We invite you to explore, learn, and grow with us.

Frequently asked questions

Can I visit the retreat?

Yes — we host open garden days in spring and autumn. Check our events calendar or contact us for details.

Do you offer workshops?

We run seasonal workshops on grafting, seed saving, and soil building. Sign up for our newsletter to stay informed.

Are your seeds available for purchase?

We offer a small selection of heritage seeds through our online store, and we participate in local seed swaps.
